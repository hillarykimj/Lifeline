How to perform CPR on an adult

If you witness a cardiac arrest, it’s crucial to call 999 and start CPR immediately. If you come across someone who is unconscious check for a response
– gently shake the person’s shoulders and ask loudly 'are you alright?'.

Someone having a cardiac arrest won’t be breathing normally or be conscious.

Keeping their head back check for regular chest movements feeling for breath on your cheek.

If the person is not breathing or not breathing normally call 999 immediately and ask for an ambulance.

Step 1

Place the heel of your hand on the breastbone at the centre of the person's chest.
Place your other hand on top of your first hand and interlock your fingers.

Step 2

Position yourself with your shoulders above your hands.

Step 3

Using your body weight (not just your arms), press straight down by 5 to 6cm (2 to 2.5 inches) on their chest.

Step 4

Keeping your hands on their chest, release the compression and allow the chest to return to its original position.

Step 5

Repeat these compressions at a rate of 100 to 120 times a minute until an ambulance arrives or you become exhausted.
